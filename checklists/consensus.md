# Checklist: Consensus

Tasks for building community consensus and collecting stakeholder signals before implementation proceeds.

## Community Discussion

- ECIP has been in Draft status for a minimum of 2 months (except in emergencies)
- Discussion thread on the ECIP PR has substantive engagement from multiple independent parties and authors
- Proposal has been presented or discussed on a community call (optional, if applicable)
- Summary of community feedback documented (supporters, concerns, objections)

## Stakeholder Outreach

- Protocol developers have reviewed and commented
- Mining pool operators have been notified and given opportunity to comment
- Major exchanges have been notified (informational — detailed coordination comes later)
- Node operators and infrastructure providers have been notified
- DApp developers affected by the changes have been consulted

## Addressing Objections

- All substantive objections documented in the ECIP PR or a linked discussion
- Objections categorized:
  - Resolved (addressed by specification changes)
  - Acknowledged (understood but not blocking)
  - Blocking (unresolved and preventing progress)
- No unresolved blocking objections remain

## Last Call

- ECIP moved to **Last Call** status by editor
- Last Call period announced on all major communication channels
- Minimum 2-month Last Call period observed
- Any final objections raised during Last Call reviewed and resolved
- Last Call period closed

## Acceptance

- Rough consensus confirmed — no significant stakeholder group is opposed
- ECIP moved to **Accepted** status by editor
- Acceptance announced on community channels

## Consensus Criteria

Rough consensus does not require unanimity. It means:

- The proposal has been openly discussed with adequate time for review
- Substantive objections have been heard and addressed
- No major stakeholder group (developers, miners, exchanges) is fundamentally opposed
- The remaining disagreements are on details, not the proposal's merit

If consensus cannot be reached, the ECIP may be:
- Revised and re-submitted for another round of review
- Deferred to a future upgrade
- Withdrawn by the author
